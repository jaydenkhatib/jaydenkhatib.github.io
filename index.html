The Players

Halifax Halifax is a city in Nova Scotia, Canada. It is the economic center of Atlantic Canada.
Boston Boston is a city in Massachusetts. It's the sixth biggest CSA in the U.S., and it's often called America's college town because it has one of the highest densities of colleges per square mile in the country.

The History

History In 1917, the SS Mont-Blanc and the SS Imo collided somewhere between upper Halifax Harbour and Bedford Basin, and the resulting explosion, the Halifax explosion, killed around 2,000 people. Boston sent money and volunteers to help rebuild Halifax. To commemorate this, Halifax sends Boston a Christmas tree each year.

The Conspiracy

The Truth While Boston did help Halifax, the Christmas tree exchange began in the 1970s as a marketing gimmick to help sell more Nova Scotian Christmas trees, according to historian Jacob Remes. Remes also explained that Halifax only started claiming that the tree exxchange was in thanks for assistance during the explosion a few years after the exchange began.
Why Lie? The Nova Scotia Christmas tree industry is worth over $30 million each year. Only Quebec (which has more tree-growing land than Nova Scotia) dedicates more land to growing Christmas trees (in Canada). Most Nova Scotian Christmas trees are shipped out of the province or to the U.S., and the anniversary of the explosion happens to be at the beginning of Holiday season. The tree's arrival is covered in Boston news stations, and they usually mention that the tree was grown in Nova Scotia. It's a perfect lie, really.
